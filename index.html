Field Notes

On why this app exists

Most fishing apps try to do too much. Social feeds, leaderboards, subscription tiers, sponsored content. Somewhere in all that noise, the simple act of keeping a fishing journal got lost.

Fishing Log is a field journal, not a social network. It's built for the angler who wants to remember where they were standing when that largemouth hit, what the water looked like, what the weather was doing. The details that a spreadsheet forgets and a photo alone can't capture.

No accounts to create. No data sold to advertisers. No monthly fees. Just a clean, reliable place to record what happened on the water today so you can be smarter about it tomorrow.

FAQ

Common questions

Does Fishing Log work without an internet connection?
Yes. Fishing Log is built offline-first. You can log catches, add photos, pin GPS spots, and record conditions even when you have zero cell signal. Everything syncs automatically when you're back online.
Is there a subscription or recurring cost?
No. Fishing Log is a one-time purchase of $4.99. You get full access to every feature with no recurring charges, no premium tiers, and no ads. Ever.
What data does Fishing Log collect about me?
Your fishing data stays on your device. We don't collect personal information, sell data to advertisers, or require an account to use the app. Your catches are yours.
Can I export my fishing data?
Absolutely. You can export your entire fishing log at any time. There's no lock-in. If you ever want to move your data somewhere else, you can take it with you.
What platforms is Fishing Log available on?
Fishing Log is available on iOS and Android. You can download it directly from the app page and start logging your catches right away.
How accurate is the GPS spot mapping?
The GPS mapping uses your device's built-in location services, which typically provide accuracy within a few meters. That's precise enough to mark specific structures, drop-offs, or shoreline positions.
Is Fishing Log open source?
Yes. You can view the source code and contribute on GitHub. We believe in transparency and building in the open.
